# Market Sophistication — Cross-Author Synthesis

## The core (Schwartz)

Markets pass through five sophistication stages: First → Bigger Claim → Mechanism → Bigger Mechanism → Saturation/Identification. Your copy strategy must match the stage. The same headline that won in Stage 1 fails in Stage 4.

See `authors/schwartz/frameworks.md` §2.

## How other authors talk about the same thing

**Brunson — "New Opportunity" vs "Improvement."** Brunson's distinction maps directly. "Improvement" sells in Stage 1-2 markets. "New Opportunity" is the Stage 5 escape hatch — when the existing market is saturated, you create a new category and start at Stage 1 of it.

**Hormozi — Mechanism via Mechanism.** When Hormozi says "give them a NEW reason why" or "a new mechanism," he's directly invoking Stage 3 sophistication strategy without naming it.

**Suby — Halo + Mechanism marketing.** Suby's content engine is itself a Stage 4-5 move — when claims are exhausted, prospects need a relationship + a reveal of the mechanism before they trust. The Halo IS sophistication-aware.

**Cialdini — Authority + Unity at Stage 4-5.** When claims no longer work, what does? Authority (credentialed mechanism), Social Proof (others did it), and Unity (you're one of us). Cialdini's principles disproportionately help in late-stage markets.

**Robbins — Identification = Stage 5.** Schwartz's Stage 5 move is "identification" with the prospect's fatigue. Robbins teaches the same — meet them in their current state, then lead.

## How to diagnose a market's sophistication stage

1. Count the major competitors and adjacent solutions the prospect has been pitched.
2. Read 10 ads in the category. What do they promise? How specific are the mechanisms?
3. Ask 5 real prospects: "What do you think when you hear [generic claim in category]?"
   - Belief + interest → Stage 1-2.
   - Skepticism but curiosity → Stage 3.
   - "I've heard that. What's the catch?" → Stage 4.
   - "Yeah, every product says that. I'm tuned out." → Stage 5.

## CombatCall worked example

**MMA betting analytics is Stage 4-5.** Evidence:
- Tons of pick services, Discord groups, "lock of the day" tweeters.
- Action Network, BetQL, Outlier, PrizePicks all exist.
- Prospects in r/sportsbook routinely roast pick services in the comments. High skepticism.
- "We have a model" is a saturated claim.

**Stage 4 plays (do these now):**
- Mechanism reveal: don't say "we have a model." Say "we score every fight on power-accuracy-weighted +EV across the last 10 years of UFC data." Specific mechanism = the Stage 3-4 unlock.
- Bigger mechanism (Stage 4): break out the components. "Three independent models — striking, grappling, situational — combined via Bayesian weight."

**Stage 5 plays (do these too):**
- Identification: "You've subscribed to three pick services. Two ghosted you after a bad month. One was a Discord guy who deleted his account. We know."
- New audience: target bettors who DON'T currently use a pick service. (Reddit lurkers, casual fans with disposable income, fantasy sports crossovers.) Those are stage 1-2 for THIS product — even if the market overall is Stage 5.
- New opportunity (Brunson move): reframe the category. Not "pick service." We're a "+EV terminal" or a "fight intelligence platform." Different category = reset to Stage 1 in the new category.

## The "reset to Stage 1" maneuver

The most powerful Stage 5 move is: don't compete in Stage 5. Reframe the offer into a new category. CombatCall isn't a "pick service" (Stage 5 saturated). It's a "fight intelligence terminal" / "UFC bettor's data co-pilot" (Stage 1 new category). New category, no competition (yet), Stage 1 copy works again.

This is also what Brunson means by "New Opportunity." It's the single most valuable move in sophisticated markets.
