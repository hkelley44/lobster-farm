# Persuasion Principles — Cross-Author Synthesis

The seven Cialdini principles, applied through the lens of every other author in this library, with CombatCall examples.

## 1. Reciprocity

**Cialdini:** give first, get later.

**Through other lenses.**
- Hormozi: lead magnets work because they trigger reciprocity.
- Suby: the entire Magic Lantern sequence is a reciprocity machine — five emails of value before one pitch.
- Brunson: free + shipping is reciprocity weaponized — they bought something low-stakes from you, now they're psychologically ready to buy more.
- Schwartz: tactical — free trial / free chapter offers as direct response.

**CombatCall.** 3-free-fights = reciprocity. Pre-card preview emails for non-subscribers = reciprocity at scale. Lean in: NAME the give. "Three picks free, every card. Forever. Even if you never pay." Saying the give out loud amplifies it.

## 2. Commitment & Consistency

**Cialdini:** small yes → large yes.

**Through other lenses.**
- Brunson: the Value Ladder IS commitment laddering. Every rung is a small yes that makes the next rung easier.
- Suby: opt-in forms with an active question ("What's your biggest betting frustration?") drive commitment.
- Hormozi: tripwire offers — get a $1 paid commitment, the $97 upsell converts dramatically better.
- Robbins: identity-based commitment is the strongest form. "I'm a data bettor" sustains behavior longer than "I bought a thing."

**CombatCall.** EmailGate could ask a commitment question. Onboarding asks the user their bankroll size + goal — both micro-commitments. Display the user's stated goal back to them on the dashboard ("You said you wanted to grow your bankroll to $X. You're at $Y.") — turns commitment into ongoing motivation.

## 3. Social Proof

**Cialdini:** uncertainty + similar others doing X → I do X.

**Through other lenses.**
- Hormozi: testimonials, case studies, specific receipts.
- Suby: long-form sales pages stacked with 10+ testimonials.
- Brunson: the entire Funnel Hacker community is social proof — see, they're all doing it.
- Schwartz: identification + social proof is the Stage 5 saturated-market unlock.

**CombatCall.** Live subscriber counter once it crosses 100. Public W/L ledger (social proof of the *product's performance*, not just users). Testimonials with screenshots and handle (with permission). "Bettor handle X, in Y state, took the underdog on UFC 300 prelim and got +218." Specific > generic.

**Caveat:** gambling is regulated. Any earnings claim must be defensible and properly disclaimered. Verify with King Arthur before shipping testimonial language about winnings.

## 4. Authority

**Cialdini:** credentials → trust.

**Through other lenses.**
- Hormozi: track record IS authority. Show the receipts.
- Schwartz: mechanism = authority in Stage 3-4 markets. "Trained on every UFC fight since 2010" is a credential.
- Brunson: "as seen on" press logos.
- Suby: King Kong's "Australia's fastest-growing agency" claim is constant authority signaling.

**CombatCall.** Mechanism details on the landing page (already partially there). Eventually: press mentions (Cole guest-on-MMA-podcasts strategy). Founder credentials (Cole's data background — make it visible). The model's transparent methodology = authority. Hidden model = looks like a scam.

## 5. Liking

**Cialdini:** we say yes to people we like.

**Through other lenses.**
- Brunson: Attractive Character. The founder is the brand.
- Robbins: rapport-building at all touchpoints.
- Suby: customer-language matching (Halo persona work).

**CombatCall.** Cole's face on the landing page. Cole's actual writing voice in the emails (not "marketing" voice). Cole as a peer, not an authority figure — he's a bettor talking to bettors.

## 6. Scarcity

**Cialdini:** less = more wanted.

**Through other lenses.**
- Hormozi: real scarcity (quantity caps) and real urgency (time caps). FAKE scarcity is the fastest trust-killer.
- Suby: launch windows, founding-member pricing, locked-in rates.
- Brunson: limited-quantity bonuses, expiring discounts.

**CombatCall.** "Founding-member rate $120/yr locked for first 500 subscribers" (REAL, finite). Event-bound: "Subscribe before UFC 300 bell to lock in for the card." Don't do fake countdown timers. Don't lie. Subscription SaaS scarcity is hard; the founding-rate move is the cleanest authentic version.

## 7. Unity

**Cialdini:** shared identity → influence.

**Through other lenses.**
- Brunson: Funnel Hackers are a tribe, Attractive Character is the chief.
- Robbins: identity-based behavior change.
- Schwartz: Stage 5 "identification" with the prospect's experience.

**CombatCall.** Build the tribe.
- Tagline: "Data bettors." OR "The +EV crew." OR "Bet from the numbers."
- Discord community for paid subscribers — instant unity moat.
- Cole writes as a member ("we're data bettors. We don't bet on hype. Here's tonight's card").
- Merch (long-term): a hat, a t-shirt, a sticker. Tribe markers.

---

## The 7-principle checklist (for any asset)

Run any marketing asset through this:

- [ ] **Reciprocity:** did I give before asking?
- [ ] **Commitment:** is there a micro-yes earlier in the path?
- [ ] **Social Proof:** is there visible evidence others have done this?
- [ ] **Authority:** is my credibility clear? Mechanism, credentials, data sources?
- [ ] **Liking:** am I a real human the prospect would like?
- [ ] **Scarcity:** is there a REAL reason to act now? (No fake timers.)
- [ ] **Unity:** does the language make them feel they're already part of something?

5+ checks → ship. Under 5 → rewrite.
