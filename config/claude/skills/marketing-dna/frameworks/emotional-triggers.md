# Emotional Triggers — Cross-Author Synthesis

Where Robbins's emotional psychology meets Cialdini's influence science. Both are about hitting the prospect's emotional state at the moment of decision.

## The shared premise

People make decisions emotionally, then justify them logically. Marketing that ignores emotion fails. Marketing that hits emotion + justifies it logically wins.

- **Robbins:** the Emotional Triad (physiology, focus, language) determines state. State drives decisions.
- **Cialdini:** the seven principles work because they bypass conscious deliberation — they ride emotional/heuristic responses.
- **Schwartz:** mass desire is emotional, not rational. You channel feelings, not facts.
- **Hormozi:** dream outcome is the feeling of arrival, not the literal product feature.
- **Brunson:** stories build belief because they bypass logical resistance.
- **Suby:** long-form copy works because it has time to take the prospect through an emotional journey.

## The emotional triggers that matter most

### 1. Fear of loss (loss aversion)

Twice as powerful as the desire for gain (Kahneman). Cialdini's Scarcity rides this. Robbins's pain-leverage rides this.

**Marketing application.** Frame outcomes as losses prevented, not just gains achieved.
- "Stop losing money to the closing line" > "Make more money."
- "Don't lose another bankroll" > "Grow your bankroll."

### 2. Belonging (Unity, Connection)

Cialdini's Unity, Robbins's Connection need.

**Marketing application.** Make the buy a tribal claim. "Data bettors don't bet on hype" — buying CombatCall = joining the tribe.

### 3. Significance

Robbins's Significance need, Cialdini's Authority-by-proxy.

**Marketing application.** Help the buyer become the smart one. The one who called it. The one with the receipt.

### 4. Certainty (in an uncertain situation)

Robbins's Certainty need, Cialdini's Authority + Social Proof.

**Marketing application.** Lower perceived risk. Mechanism + guarantee + social proof + transparent track record. The buyer wants to STOP feeling uncertain.

### 5. Curiosity (open loops)

Brunson's hook mechanics, Schwartz's headline patterns. Curiosity is the bridge from "scrolling past" to "reading the next sentence."

**Marketing application.** Open loops in headlines. "The one fight-stat that predicts 73% of UFC bets — and why it's not striking accuracy." Curiosity pulls the click.

### 6. Hope (renewed)

Schwartz's "mass desire" channeled forward. The Magic Lantern works because each step renews hope that THIS time is different.

**Marketing application.** Position the offer as the renewed-hope path. "You've tried pick services and got burned. This is different — here's why."

### 7. Anger (toward a shared enemy)

Brunson's polarity. Cialdini's Unity (the in-group needs an out-group).

**Marketing application.** Name the enemy. NOT competitors directly (legal + tasteless). Name the abstract enemy.
- CombatCall enemies: "lock of the day" tweeters with no track record. Discord scammers. Sportsbooks that limit winning action. Hype-betting your hard-earned money.

### 8. Pride (anticipated)

Future-state pride. "Imagine yourself...". Robbins's pleasure-leverage.

**Marketing application.** Paint the post-purchase moment cinematically. "Sunday morning, green ledger, screenshot in the group chat, the friend who roasted you for subscribing now asking how he can get in."

## Pairing emotional triggers with offer components

| Offer component | Primary emotion |
|---|---|
| Headline / hook | Curiosity + (loss-fear OR pride) |
| Story / lead | Hope + identification |
| Mechanism reveal | Certainty |
| Stack of value | Significance ("look what I'm getting") |
| Bonuses | Greed + certainty (objection-killer) |
| Guarantee | Certainty (risk reversal) |
| Scarcity / urgency | Loss-fear |
| Social proof | Belonging + certainty |
| Final CTA | Pride (anticipated) |

## How to run this on any asset

Walk paragraph by paragraph. Ask: "What is the prospect FEELING here?" If the answer is "nothing in particular," cut or rewrite. Every paragraph earns an emotion or earns the cut.

## CombatCall emotional arc example (long-form sales page)

1. Hero: curiosity + loss-fear. "If you bet UFC, you're probably leaking money you don't know about."
2. Story (Cole's): identification + hope. "I bought picks from tipsters. They were guessing — gut feeling dressed up as analysis. So I built my own model."
3. Pain (your story): loss-fear + frustration. "Every card it's the same — you bet the favorite, the upset hits, you tell yourself next time."
4. Mechanism reveal: certainty. "Three independent models — striking, grappling, situational — trained on every fight since 2010."
5. Stack: significance + greed. "Here's everything you get."
6. Guarantee: certainty. "Three free fights, every card, forever. You decide if it's worth $120/yr."
7. Social proof: belonging. "We're data bettors. We have receipts."
8. CTA: pride (future) + curiosity (will it work for me?). "See the next card's picks Saturday morning. Sign up now."
