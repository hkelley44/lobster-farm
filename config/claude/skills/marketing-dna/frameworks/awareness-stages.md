# Awareness Stages — Cross-Author Synthesis

## The core (Schwartz)

Five stages of prospect awareness — Unaware → Problem-Aware → Solution-Aware → Product-Aware → Most-Aware. Each demands a different copy strategy.

See `authors/schwartz/frameworks.md` §1.

## How other authors talk about the same thing

**Hormozi — implicit in offer language.** Hormozi doesn't formalize awareness stages but his copy is calibrated to Stage 2-3 (problem-aware shopping for solutions). His hook-style headlines work because they match the awareness of his cold-to-warm traffic.

**Suby — the Magic Lantern Technique.** Suby's Magic Lantern is literally the operational implementation of moving someone up the awareness ladder. Each email/touchpoint nudges them one stage closer to "most-aware."

**Brunson — Epiphany Bridge.** Brunson's Epiphany Bridge is a Stage-1-or-2 unlock — it moves someone from "I didn't know this was a problem / didn't believe a solution existed" to "I see the opportunity." Then HSO carries them through Stage 3-5.

**Cialdini — Pre-Suasion is a stage-shifter.** Cialdini's pre-suasive openers can lift a prospect's awareness state in seconds by directing attention. "Quick question: how much did you lose on UFC last year?" pre-suades by surfacing problem-awareness before the offer.

**Robbins — pain-pleasure surfaces Stage 1.** Robbins's pain-leverage moves are designed to surface unconscious dissatisfaction — moving an Unaware prospect to Problem-Aware via emotional contrast.

## Mapping traffic sources to awareness stages

| Traffic source | Likely stage | Implication for copy |
|---|---|---|
| Cold paid ads (interest targeting) | Stage 1-2 | Lead with problem agitation or pattern interrupt. Don't sell product yet. |
| Retargeting paid ads | Stage 2-4 | Lead with mechanism + social proof. |
| Organic content (X thread, YouTube) | Stage 1-3 | Range. Headline determines who clicks. Use the click as a stage filter. |
| Direct search ("UFC betting tool") | Stage 3-4 | Lead with product-specific value prop + mechanism. |
| Search for brand name | Stage 4-5 | Lead with offer + price + risk reversal. |
| Existing email list | Stage 4-5 | Lead with deal, urgency, scarcity. |
| Word-of-mouth referral | Stage 3-5 | Lead with social proof (the referrer) and offer. |

## CombatCall worked example

**combatcall.com landing page today** is serving traffic from at least 3 different sources at once (organic, X clicks, direct). Headlines that work for ALL stages are rare. The honest fix: separate landing pages per source, or accept that the homepage will be Stage 2-3 oriented (the median) and let outliers either bounce or auto-progress via the EmailGate funnel.

**Recommendation:**
- Keep `combatcall.com` as the Stage 2-3 page (problem-aware shopping for a solution).
- Build a Stage 1 lander for cold paid traffic: `combatcall.com/are-you-losing` (problem-surfacing pre-suasion → sign up to find out).
- Build a Stage 4-5 lander for retargeting + direct search: `combatcall.com/start` (offer-led, mechanism shown, immediate signup).

## Heuristic: how to tell which stage your prospect is at

Ask: if you handed the prospect a microphone, what would they say?
- "I don't bet on UFC and don't care" → Unaware. Wrong audience entirely.
- "I bet on UFC but lose money and don't know what to do" → Problem-aware.
- "I bet on UFC, lose money, and have considered pick services but they all seem shady" → Solution-aware.
- "I bet on UFC, lose money, considered services, found yours but haven't pulled the trigger" → Product-aware.
- "I've used CombatCall for three months and I'm deciding whether to renew" → Most-aware.

Copy that lands at the wrong stage feels weird to the prospect. They can't articulate why; they just bounce.
