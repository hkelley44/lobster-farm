## The Mechanism

```
Most pick services are a black box. Trust them, or don't.

CombatCall isn't. Here's exactly how the model works:

THREE INDEPENDENT SCORES, COMBINED.

  1. STRIKING SCORE.
     Power-accuracy ratio (significant strikes landed × power impact),
     decayed by recency, adjusted for opponent caliber via Elo-style ranking.
     This is NOT "strikes per minute." Power-weighting matters and most
     public models skip it.

  2. GRAPPLING SCORE.
     Control time × submission attempt rate, normalized to weight class.
     Catches the underrated grapplers most services miss.

  3. SITUATIONAL SCORE.
     Camp changes, weight cuts, ring rust, cage size, prelim vs main,
     short-notice acceptance. The intangibles, quantified.

Each fight gets three scores. They get weighted by fight type (stand-up
vs grappling vs intangible-driven). Then we cross-reference current odds
for +EV.

That's the pick.

  → Trained on every UFC fight since 2010
  → ~5,200 fights of data
  → Every pick output is auditable, with the math, on every fight page
  → Public W/L ledger at combatcall.com/picks
```
