# Alex Hormozi — Frameworks

## 1. The Value Equation

> Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

**What it is.** A four-variable equation for how a prospect actually perceives the value of an offer. Numerator drivers create value; denominator drivers destroy it. Hormozi's claim: most marketers obsess over the numerator (bigger promises, more guarantees). The leverage is usually in the denominator — collapse time-to-result and reduce effort/sacrifice, and value explodes.

**The four variables.**
1. **Dream Outcome** — what the customer experiences when their dream comes true. NOT the product. ("Lose 20 pounds and look great in a swimsuit," not "gym membership.")
2. **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** — how believable it is to THEM that they'll get the outcome. Proof, mechanism, guarantees, testimonials, free trials all move this.
3. **Time Delay** — wall-clock time between purchase and the outcome (or first taste of it). Faster = more valuable. Front-load wins.
4. **Effort & Sacrifice** — the energy + the things they have to give up. "Done for you" beats "done with you" beats "done by you" on this axis.

**When to use it.** Auditing any offer that isn't converting. Score each variable 1-10. The lowest score is where to invest. Also: deciding what to put on a landing page above the fold (you're maximizing perceived value in 5 seconds — that means hitting Dream Outcome + Likelihood hard).

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Dream Outcome: "Win more of your UFC bets / stop bleeding money to Vegas." Don't sell picks. Sell winning bets.
- Perceived Likelihood: weakest variable today. Fix with verified track record, fighter-data screenshots, public win/loss receipts, and "no-pick-without-the-numbers" mechanism positioning.
- Time Delay: short. Next fight night = the moment of truth. Use this. ("48 hours from now you'll know if I'm full of it.")
- Effort: low. Sign up, read the card, place bets. Lean into that — "the work is done, you just read the card."

**Good vs bad.**
- Bad: "AI-powered UFC analytics" (generic, doesn't move any variable).
- Good: "We analyzed every UFC fight since 2010. You get three free fights every card — verified picks, with the data behind them. If they hit, you stay. If they don't, you walk."

**Source.** $100M Offers, Ch. 5 ("The Value Equation"). Hormozi has also taught it in `acquisition.com/training/offers4`.

## 2. The Grand Slam Offer (Five-Step Creation)

**What it is.** Hormozi's process for building an offer "so good people feel stupid saying no." It's the workflow that operationalizes the Value Equation.

**The steps.**
1. **Identify the Dream Outcome.** What does the customer ACTUALLY want when they buy? (Not what they say. What they want.)
2. **List every obstacle** they hit before, during, and after using your product. Brain-dump 20+. Each one is a future bonus or guarantee.
3. **Reverse problems into solutions** — turn each obstacle into a deliverable. "Doesn't know how to start" → "step-by-step onboarding video."
4. **Determine delivery vehicles** — for each solution, what's the highest-value way to deliver it? Hormozi's "Delivery Cube": dimensions are (a) personal attention 1:1 → 1:many, (b) effort by you vs them, (c) medium (text/audio/video/live), (d) format, (e) response speed.
5. **Trim and stack.** Cut the low-value high-cost deliverables. Stack the rest into one bundle. Show the stacked value vs the price. (Hormozi's gym example: $4,351 stacked value, $599 price.)

**When to use it.** Standing up a new offer from scratch. Re-architecting an existing offer that's converting poorly.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Step 1: "I want to make money betting on UFC."
- Step 2 obstacles: (a) don't know which fights are good bets, (b) don't have data on fighters, (c) can't tell hype from real edge, (d) fear of losing $, (e) don't trust pick services, (f) bankroll discipline, (g) sports books minimize action.
- Step 3 solutions: (a) verified pick of the week, (b) full fighter dossier per matchup, (c) "edge score" w/ historical data, (d) free 3 fights/event to prove value first, (e) public track record, (f) bankroll guide, (g) +EV bet sizing math.
- Step 4: most are app features. Bonus material as PDFs (low cost, high perceived value).
- Step 5: stack on the paywall page. "$120/year. Comes with: verified picks every event ($600/yr value if from a tout). Full fighter dossiers ($240). Edge-score model ($300). Bankroll guide. UFC Hall of Fame pick archive."

**Source.** $100M Offers, Ch. 4-8.

## 3. The MAGIC Naming Formula

**What it is.** Five-component formula for naming an offer or product. Use 3-5 of the 5 components.

**Components.**
- **M — Magnet (Magnetic reason why):** seasonal/themed hook ("Summer Cut Challenge")
- **A — Avatar:** who it's for, exclusionary ("For Fighters Over 35")
- **G — Goal:** the dream outcome ("Drop 10lbs")
- **I — Interval:** time frame ("in 6 Weeks")
- **C — Container:** the wrapper word — Challenge, Bootcamp, Blueprint, Accelerator, System, Method

**Applied — CombatCall.**

- "The Fight Night Edge Report" (Goal + Container, evergreen)

## 4. The Core Four Lead Channels

**What it is.** Per Hormozi: there are ONLY four ways to advertise. Master all four and you have infinite leverage.

**The four.**
1. **Warm Outreach** — people who know you (network, past customers, followers). Highest conversion. Lowest scale. Start here.
2. **Cold Outreach** — strangers you reach 1:1 (cold email, DMs, cold calls). Medium scale. Demands personalization.
3. **Posting Free Content** — public, scaled, slow-burn. Highest long-term ROI. Compounds.
4. **Paid Ads** — buy attention. Fastest scale. Demands a proven offer.

**Sequence.** Start warm + content. Add cold outreach. Add paid ads ONLY once warm/content have proven the offer converts. Paid ads on a broken offer = setting money on fire faster.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Warm: King Arthur's brother + advisor (existing). Personal post on Cole's X. Friends in MMA Twitter.
- Cold: DM strangers on r/MMAbetting, Twitter MMA bettors who post W/L records. Offer free 3-fight trial (the gate is already built — it IS the lead magnet).
- Content: Cole posts pick threads on X every UFC card. Reddit fight breakdown posts. YouTube fight-card preview videos. THIS is the long-term engine.
- Paid: skip until warm+content prove the funnel converts. Then Meta + YouTube ads with a verified-track-record creative.

**Source.** $100M Leads, Ch. 6-9. The "Core Four" is the spine of the entire book.

## 5. The Lead Magnet Formula

**What it is.** A free thing (digital or physical) that solves a narrow problem completely and naturally leads to your paid offer.

**Three types of lead magnets.**
1. **Reveal a problem** they didn't know they had ("Find the 7 Holes in Your Bankroll Strategy").
2. **Sample/free trial** of the paid thing (CombatCall's 3-free-fights IS this).
3. **Give one step of a multi-step solution** ("The Pre-Fight Checklist — Step 1 of the +EV System").

**6 (or 7) steps to build one.**
1. Pick the problem to solve.
2. Pick how to solve it (which of the 3 types).
3. Pick how to deliver it (PDF, video, calc, swipe file, checklist).
4. Test the name. (Use the Lead Magnet Headline formula: `[Number] + [Adjective] + [Target Audience] + [Outcome] + [Time Frame]`. Example: "7 Proven Strategies for UFC Bettors to Beat the Closing Line in 30 Days.")
5. Make it easy to consume (short, scannable).
6. Make it darn good (overdeliver).

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- The freemium 3-fights gate IS the sample lead magnet. Position it as such on the landing page.
- Additional lead magnet ideas: "The UFC Bettor's Cheat Sheet — 12 Stats That Predict 73% of Fights" (PDF). "Fight Night Bankroll Calc" (free tool). "Last 5 Cards Reviewed: What Worked, What Didn't" (free email).

**Source.** $100M Leads, Section II.

## 6. The Four Guarantees

**What it is.** Hormozi catalogs four guarantee structures. Each handles risk differently.

1. **Unconditional** — "Hate it? Full refund, no questions." Strongest reversal of risk. Best for high-trust markets.
2. **Conditional** — "Refund IF you've done X, Y, Z." Filters refund abusers; raises commitment.
3. **Anti-guarantee** — "All sales final. We're THAT confident this works." Reverse psychology. Works for premium offers.
4. **Implied** — "If you don't make money, we don't make money." Performance-based. Strongest credibility.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Conditional: "If you don't profit on your first month following our verified picks (placed via your linked book), we refund the month — bring receipts." (Note: needs care w/ gambling regulators — confirm legality before shipping.)
- Implied (soft): "Three free fights every card. If our calls aren't better than your gut by Fight #3, don't pay us."
- Anti-guarantee: probably skip — too edgy for early-stage trust-building.

**Source.** $100M Offers, Ch. 16-18.

## 7. Scarcity + Urgency (the math)

- **Scarcity** = limit on quantity ("only 100 spots").
- **Urgency** = limit on time ("ends Friday").

**Hormozi's rule:** if you use them, they must be real. Fake scarcity is the fastest way to permanently kill trust on a list.

**Applied — CombatCall.** Hard to use authentically in subscription SaaS. Lean on event-driven urgency instead: "UFC 300 cards drop Wed. Free 3-fight preview goes live Thursday. Subscribe before fight night to unlock the full card."

**Source.** $100M Offers, Ch. 19-20.

## 8. Money Models (from $100M Money Models, 2025)

**What it is.** Hormozi's newer framework — the offer + the *sequence* of offers + the *payment mechanics* that turn a one-time buyer into long-term cashflow.

**Key concept: Client-Financed Acquisition (CFA).** Structure your front-end offer so that the first 30 days of revenue from a new customer covers the cost of acquiring them + the next one. This makes ad spend self-funding and scaling unconstrained.

**Implication for CombatCall.** Annual upfront ($120) is materially better than monthly ($25) for CFA — you collect a year of revenue on day 1 instead of bleeding it out monthly. Push the annual plan harder in checkout. A "first month $1" trip-wire is another CFA pattern worth testing.

**Source.** $100M Money Models (2025) — see `100m-money-models-affiliate-blackbook.pdf` and `money-models-new-secret-chapter.pdf` (download URLs in `sources.md`).

## 10. Client-Financed Acquisition Refined — The 30-Day Rule

This is the spine of *$100M Money Models* and the post-2024 Hormozi obsession.

**The rule.** Recoup your customer acquisition cost (CAC) within the first 30 days of customer revenue. Not 90 days. Not over LTV. **30 days.**

**Why 30.** Cashflow recycles monthly. If you recoup CAC in 30 days, you can reinvest the same dollar into the NEXT customer 12x per year — without external capital. CFA at 30 days = unconstrained scaling.

**The mechanics for CombatCall.**
- Annual plan = $120 collected on day 1.
- If CAC is ≤ $120, you're 30-day-positive on annual subscribers.
- Monthly plan = $25 collected on day 1. If CAC is > $25, you're underwater for at least one month. Scaling is constrained.
- **Implication:** ANNUAL PLAN IS THE PRIMARY OFFER. Monthly is the safety net. Push annual hard at the paywall.

**The leverage moves.**
- Add an immediate upsell at checkout (e.g., $40 "Bankroll Calculator + Hall of Fame archive") — increases first-30-day revenue without increasing CAC.
- Add a downsell on annual decline ($25 monthly).
- Add a "first 30 days bonus" for annual purchasers — paid for by the higher LTV.

**Source.** $100M Money Models PDF (download URL in `sources.md`) + Diary of a CEO interview. The Black Friday 2024 X post launched this framework publicly.

## 12. The Fulcrum Model — Supply × Demand × Leverage

Hormozi's stated thesis for acquisition.com. The acquisition.com logo IS this model — a triangle/fulcrum.

**Three foundational elements of any business:**
1. **Supply** — your capacity to deliver.
2. **Demand** — your market's willingness to pay.
3. **Leverage** — the multiplier that turns input into disproportionate output.

**Three levels of business function:**
1. Attract attention.
2. Convert attention to customer.
3. Deliver on the promise.

**The rule.** Each function should be designed for MAXIMUM LEVERAGE — lowest time/$ input for highest output.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Attract: content (high leverage) > cold DMs (low leverage). Cole's threads = 1:thousands. DMs = 1:1.
- Convert: video sales letter (high leverage) > sales calls (low leverage). The product converts itself if the offer is right.
- Deliver: software (highest leverage). Picks generated by automated model = 1:infinity.

**Hormozi's implicit warning.** If any function is low-leverage and the founder is the bottleneck, that's where scaling will stall. Identify the bottleneck function. Add leverage.

**Source.** Diary of a CEO.

## 13. The 3D Training Framework — Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate

For training anyone on anything.

1. **Document** — write the job into a checklist. (This step exposes everything you didn't know was implicit knowledge.)
2. **Demonstrate** — perform the task yourself in front of them. (This exposes everything the checklist missed.)
3. **Duplicate** — they perform it in front of you. Correct in real time. Repeat until pattern is locked.

**Why it matters for Tristan.** When CombatCall eventually hires a marketing junior (or Cole brings on a content creator), this is how Tristan trains them. Also how Tristan trains the OTHER agents to ship Tristan-grade copy.

**Source.** Diary of a CEO.

## 14. The Stop / Start / Keep Feedback Framework

For correcting behavior without insulting.

After any output (a piece of copy, a campaign, a hire's first week), feedback in three buckets:
- **Stop** doing X.
- **Start** doing Y.
- **Keep** doing Z.

Format each one as a specific BEHAVIOR + a specific CONDITION. Not a judgment.

**Bad:** "Your copy is weak." (Insult.)
**Good:** "Stop opening landing pages with the product name. Start with the prospect's pain. Keep using the second-person voice." (Behaviors.)

**Source.** Diary of a CEO.

## 15. The Barrels and Ammunition Model (Keith Rabois, cited by Hormozi)

Most people are AMMUNITION — they execute defined work.
Few people are BARRELS — they CREATE new directions and rate-limit the company.

**The rule.** Organizations scale on barrels, not ammunition. Hire ammo to support each barrel. Identify your barrels and remove their constraints.

**The square root law.** In any 100-person company, ~10 people generate ~50% of the value. Find them. Protect them.

**Applied — CombatCall.** Cole is the founder-barrel. Tristan should treat himself as a barrel for marketing — generating new campaigns, creative angles, market tests — not as ammunition executing predefined briefs. If Tristan is operating in "execution-only" mode for too many sessions in a row, that's a signal that the barrel/ammunition ratio is wrong.

**Source.** Diary of a CEO, citing Keith Rabois.

## 16. The Smallest Skill Deficiency Hiring Heuristic

When hiring, optimize for the EASIEST GAP TO CLOSE.

- **Low-skilled roles:** hire for attitude (hard to train). Train aptitude (easy to train).
- **High-skilled roles:** hire for the smallest skill gap you can find. If attitude needs work but skill is rare, take the skill — attitude is sometimes easier to coach than expert-level technical depth.

**Applied — CombatCall.** Not directly marketing-relevant, but useful when Cole hires the next contributor. Tell him: don't hire on charisma if the role is highly technical; don't hire on technical depth if the role is largely relational. Match the gap.

**Source.** Diary of a CEO.

## 17. The Audience 20/60/20 Rule

For brand and content strategy.

For any audience you build:
- **20%** will love you no matter what. Superfans. Amplifiers.
- **60%** are reasonable and quiet. They consume but rarely engage publicly. The silent majority who actually buys.
- **20%** will reject you no matter what. The haters.

**Hormozi's rule.** Never dilute yourself to win over the 20% rejectors. You can't. Diluting only loses the 20% superfans and bores the 60% silent majority. **Serve the 60% by being uncompromisingly the version of you that the 20% superfans love.**

**Quote (attributed to Layla Hormozi):** *"Never dilute yourself."*

**Applied — CombatCall.** Cole writes the way Cole writes. Tristan amplifies — never sands down — the polarizing edges. The bettors who say "this guy is too data-pilled" are the 20% rejectors. Don't write for them.

**Source.** Diary of a CEO.

## 18. The Vague-to-Specific Decision Framework

For overcoming fear-based decision paralysis. Cole-relevant for any "should I post this / ship this / spend this" moment.

**The pattern.** Fear is amygdala-driven and VAGUE — "this might go badly." To dissolve it, translate vague into SPECIFIC:
- Worst case scenario, in literal detail. What exactly happens?
- Likelihood of that scenario, honestly.
- Recovery path from that scenario, in concrete steps.

Once the worst case is named, sized, and survivable, the amygdala lets go.

**Layla Hormozi's quote (from interview):** *"Fear is a mile wide, an inch deep."*

**Applied — Tristan.** Use this when reviewing copy that feels "too edgy." Ask: what's the literal worst case if we ship this? Usually: 0.5% unsubscribe, 1 angry tweet, 0 lost revenue. Ship.

**Source.** Diary of a CEO.

## 19. The Brand-as-Mosaic Theory

Hormozi's content strategy in one image.

**The model.** Your brand is a MOSAIC. Each piece of content is one TILE. Each tile is a single color. From close up, no individual tile reveals the picture. From far away (1,000 tiles), the unique pattern emerges.

**Five brand components Hormozi uses for himself:**
1. Philosophy
2. Business/marketing
3. Fitness
4. Family/relationships
5. Humor/personality

**The leverage.** The PROPORTIONS of each color vary by creator. That's what makes the mosaic unique. Imitate someone else's TOPICS and your mosaic looks identical. Imitate their PROPORTIONS and it doesn't work either. The mix has to be yours.

**Applied — CombatCall (Cole's mosaic candidates):**
- Pick threads + analysis (40% — the substance)
- Bankroll war stories (20% — vulnerability + Attractive Character)
- MMA fan content unrelated to betting (15% — the fan-not-tout signal)
- Founder/build-in-public posts (15% — the lived experience)
- Humor + meme-aware MMA Twitter participation (10% — likability)

The OUTPUT is unique because the PROPORTIONS are Cole's.

## 21. Specificity Attracts (The Polarity Rule)

Brand-building principle, drawn from the Nike "What's yours is mine" ad cited by Hormozi.

**The rule.** Drawing a line — standing AGAINST something specific — attracts true believers and repels people who weren't going to convert anyway. Vanilla messaging attracts no one in particular.

**Applied — CombatCall.** What CombatCall stands AGAINST is the differentiator:

- Against "lock of the day" Twitter cappers who delete the L's.
- Against picks-by-vibes and gut.
- Against sportsbooks that minimize action on winners.

Lead with these in social content. The bettors who hate Discord tout scammers are the bettors who already understand what CombatCall is for.

## 22. The Consultant Learning Method

A Hormozi research-protocol. Useful when Tristan needs to rapidly skill-up on a new domain (e.g., a new ad platform, a new copywriting niche).

1. Find 5 experts in the field.
2. Ask each for 5 more names. (Now you have ~25 candidates.)
3. Take raw notes from each. Organize by theme.
4. Consolidate into a few core truths.
5. Generate inferences + your own POV.

You now know more than 99% of people in the field — in days, not years.

**Source.** Diary of a CEO. Hormozi credits this method for how he built his own offer-design expertise.
