# Alex Hormozi — Key Concepts + Vocabulary

## Vocabulary you'll see Hormozi use

- **Grand Slam Offer** — an offer good enough that prospects feel dumb saying no. Always paired w/ the Value Equation.
- **Dream Outcome** — the end-state customer experience, not your product.
- **Mechanism** — the HOW you deliver. The "secret sauce" that justifies the claim. ("We don't just count strikes, we weight them by power-accuracy ratio across the last 3 fights.")
- **Delivery Cube** — 5 axes for choosing HOW you deliver a solution (1:1 vs 1:many, your effort vs theirs, medium, format, response time).
- **Trim & Stack** — cut low-value high-cost deliverables, bundle the rest, show stacked value > price.
- **Core Four** — warm outreach, cold outreach, content, paid ads. The only four ways.
- **CFA / Client-Financed Acquisition** — front-end revenue ≥ cost to acquire = unlimited ad budget.
- **LTGP:CAC** — Lifetime Gross Profit to Customer Acquisition Cost. He targets 3:1 minimum, 5:1 for healthy scaling.
- **The 4 Types of Buyer** — XYAB framework. Not the same as awareness; about price tier and commitment.
- **Lead Magnet** — narrow problem, complete solution, free.
- **The "More" Method** — how to ask for more leads from existing channels before opening new ones.

## Thinking patterns

- **Math beats magic.** Every framework reduces to a number you can measure. "Value Equation" is literally an equation.
- **Operator > marketer.** Hormozi thinks like someone running 50 gyms (because he did). Marketing is a means to LTV:CAC math.
- **Volume + clarity > cleverness.** "Do more, more simply." His copy is plain-spoken on purpose.
- **The 1-in-100 leverage point.** Most things don't matter much. Find the one thing that 10x's the equation. Usually it's the offer, not the copy.
- **"They don't buy what you sell, they buy what they want."** Sell the dream outcome, deliver the product.
- **Sequence beats intensity.** $100M Money Models is the formalization — the sequence of offers matters more than any single one.

## Hormozi's biases to be aware of

- Bias toward direct-response service businesses (gyms, agencies, info products). Less natural fit for pure SaaS — though his frameworks port fine, the math examples don't always translate.
- Bias toward solo founders & small teams. He underweights brand/enterprise dynamics.
- Bias toward "tell people what they want, then deliver the goods" — works in commodity markets, can underdeliver in identity-driven markets (which is where Brunson is stronger).

## Where to use Hormozi specifically

- New offer design (he's #1 here).
- Pricing/packaging audit.
- Lead-gen channel decisions.
- Headlines and naming.
- Anytime someone says "should I increase the price?" — go straight to Value Equation.

## Where NOT to use Hormozi alone

- Identity / movement / story-driven brands → call in Brunson.
- Influence psychology → call in Cialdini.
- Emotional state / decision-making → call in Robbins.
- Highly sophisticated saturated markets → Schwartz's sophistication ladder beats Hormozi's "make a better offer" because at Stage 4-5 a better offer alone gets ignored.
