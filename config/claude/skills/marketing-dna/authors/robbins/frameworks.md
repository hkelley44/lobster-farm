# Tony Robbins — Frameworks

Tony Robbins doesn't write marketing books. He writes about human behavior. But his frameworks are foundational for understanding WHY people buy — the emotional + identity engines under every purchase decision. Use Robbins when you need to understand the prospect's internal state, motivation, and decision-making.

## 1. The Six Human Needs

**What it is.** Robbins's claim: every human behavior is driven by an attempt to meet one or more of six fundamental needs. Every person prioritizes them differently. The first four are needs of personality (everyone meets them somehow, healthy or not). The last two are needs of spirit (you have to choose to pursue them).

**The six.**
1. **Certainty** — safety, predictability, comfort, control.
2. **Variety / Uncertainty** — novelty, adventure, surprise, change.
3. **Significance** — feeling important, unique, special, needed.
4. **Love & Connection** — belonging, intimacy, being seen.
5. **Growth** — expansion, learning, becoming more.
6. **Contribution** — giving back, leaving a legacy, serving something bigger than self.

**The paradox:** certainty and variety are in tension. So are significance and connection. The same product can be sold to meet either side, and most people are unconsciously trying to balance both.

**Marketing application.** Figure out which 2-3 needs your offer hits. Then the copy speaks to those needs *explicitly* — not the product features.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- **Significance** (BIG) — bettors want to be the smart one. The one who called Tank Davis vs Ryan Garcia. Copy that lets them imagine showing the screenshot to the group chat sells.
- **Certainty** — they want CERTAINTY they're not betting blind. Data, mechanism, track record all hit this.
- **Variety** — fight nights ARE the variety hit. Lean into "every Saturday is the next test."
- **Connection (subtle)** — being part of an in-group of "real" bettors. Discord community plays here (future Value Ladder rung).
- **Growth** — for the serious bettors, the data is the education. "I'm becoming a better bettor."

Copy to write: "You don't want to gamble. You want to *know.*" — speaks to Certainty + Significance simultaneously.

**Source.** Robbins's Unleash the Power Within seminars. Strategic Intervention training. https://www.tonyrobbins.com/six-human-needs-test/

## 2. The Emotional Triad — Physiology / Focus / Language

**What it is.** Three levers that determine your emotional state at any moment. Change any one, you change your state. Change all three, you change instantly.

- **Physiology** — posture, breathing, movement, facial expression.
- **Focus** — what you're paying attention to. "Where focus goes, energy flows."
- **Language** — the words you say to yourself + others about the situation.

**Marketing application.** This is the LEVERAGE point for landing-page UX + ad design. Your job is to put the prospect in a specific emotional state before the ask.

- Physiology — you can't control their body, BUT video creative can. A high-energy hype video shifts physiology. A calm explainer shifts it differently.
- Focus — you control this absolutely. Your hook is what they focus on. Make it count.
- Language — you can plant words in their head that they then think back at themselves. Schwartz and Cialdini both knew this; Robbins formalized it.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- The hero video (already exists: `landing-hero-video.mp4`) shifts physiology — fight clips get the body amped.
- Focus: the hero headline tells them WHAT to focus on for the next 30 seconds. Make it the dream outcome, not the product.
- Language: seed phrases they'll re-use mentally. "Bet from data, not from hype." That phrase becomes their identity statement.

**Source.** Robbins, Awaken the Giant Within (1991). https://www.tonyrobbins.com/blog/discover-your-peak-state

## 3. Pain-Pleasure Leverage

**What it is.** Robbins's most quoted idea: humans are wired to move away from pain and toward pleasure. The pain of staying the same MUST exceed the pain of changing for someone to act. And the pleasure of the future state MUST be vividly imagined.

**Marketing application.** Sales pages do this in sequence:
1. Open the wound (current pain — make it tangible).
2. Twist the knife (consequence of NOT acting — future pain).
3. Paint the dream (future pleasure of acting — make it cinematic).
4. Present the bridge (your offer).

This is the spine of every direct-response sales page going back to Halbert and Schwartz, but Robbins gave it the psychological language.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Pain: "You watched your bet evaporate in round 1 because you 'felt' good about a heavy favorite."
- Future pain: "Next card is in 5 days. Are you going to do the same thing? How much further does the bankroll drop before you stop?"
- Pleasure: "Imagine pulling up your phone Sunday morning to a green ledger. The screenshot you finally get to post."
- Bridge: "Three verified picks every card. Free for the first three fights. $25/mo if it works."

**Source.** Robbins, Awaken the Giant Within (1991), Ch. 6.

## 4. RPM — Rapid Planning Method (Result / Purpose / Massive Action)

**What it is.** Robbins's planning system. Most people start with tasks. RPM flips: start with the RESULT, then the PURPOSE (why it matters), then the MASSIVE ACTION PLAN (the tasks).

**Marketing application.** This is less about copy and more about Tristan's own work + how marketing campaigns are planned.

- For every campaign brief: write the Result first (e.g., "300 paid signups in 30 days"). Then Purpose ("validates the funnel for Meta paid scale"). Then the action plan.
- Embed in template: every ad campaign doc starts with R/P/MAP.

**Source.** https://www.tonyrobbins.com/events/rapid-planning-method

## 5. The Power of Decision (and Identity)

**What it is.** Robbins's claim that the moment of decision is the moment of change — and decision is downstream of identity. People act consistent with who they believe they are. Change the identity, change the behavior.

**Marketing application.** This is what Brunson stole and called "new opportunity / new identity." Robbins is the deeper source.

- Position your customer's purchase as an IDENTITY claim. "I'm someone who bets from data" is identity. "I bought CombatCall" is a transaction.
- Onboarding emails reinforce the new identity. ("Welcome to CombatCall. You just stopped being a hype-bettor.")

**Applied — CombatCall.** Welcome email subject: "You're a data bettor now." That's the new identity claim. Everything downstream reinforces it.

**Source.** Awaken the Giant Within, Ch. 5 ("The Power of Decision").

## 6. Anchoring (NLP-flavored)

**What it is.** Pavlovian — pair an emotional state with a stimulus (sight, sound, phrase, gesture). Repeat. Eventually the stimulus alone triggers the state.

**Marketing application.** Brand assets are anchors.
- A specific color (CombatCall's `#f2c94c` gold).
- A specific phrase (a tagline used relentlessly).
- A specific founder gesture or phrase.
- A specific sound effect (the cha-ching of a winning bet, in a video ad).

Done right, prospects feel something specific when they see your brand.

**Source.** Robbins's NLP-origin work; Awaken the Giant Within. (NLP itself is contested as a research field but the anchoring intuition holds up in marketing practice.)
