# Robert Cialdini — Key Concepts + Vocabulary

## Vocabulary

- **Seven Principles** — Reciprocity, Commitment/Consistency, Social Proof, Authority, Liking, Scarcity, Unity.
- **Pre-Suasion** — the act of preparing attention before the ask.
- **Privileged Moment** — the window in which a pre-suasive opener has heightened effect.
- **Channeled Attention** — the cognitive bias that whatever you're focused on gets over-weighted in decisions.
- **Click, Whir** — Cialdini's metaphor for the auto-pilot persuasion response.
- **Fixed-Action Pattern** — instinctive responses triggered by specific cues. Many persuasion principles ride on these.
- **Contrast Principle** — perception is relative; a $1,000 anchor makes $200 look cheap.
- **Door-in-the-Face / Foot-in-the-Door** — rejection-then-retreat technique vs small-yes-then-big-ask. Both leverage commitment/consistency.
- **Lowball** — establish commitment, then change terms. Cialdini documented but condemns.
- **Liking → Familiarity, Similarity, Compliments, Cooperation, Conditioning, Physical Attractiveness** — the six liking sub-drivers.

## Thinking patterns

- **Influence is mostly automatic.** Most decisions are made by heuristics, not deliberation. You design for the heuristic, not the conscious mind.
- **Context > content.** The same words land differently depending on what came right before. Hence Pre-Suasion.
- **Ethics matter or it backfires.** Manipulation gets discovered, trust evaporates, the principle inverts. The smart play is Sleuth, not Smuggler.
- **Academic rigor.** Cialdini is the only one of the six who's a scientist by trade. His claims are research-backed. When you cite him, you cite credibility.

## Cialdini's biases to be aware of

- Less actionable in execution than Hormozi/Suby/Brunson. He gives you the principles; you have to do the translation to copy.
- More B2C consumer-decision focused than B2B.
- Pre-Suasion is more theoretical than Influence. Less immediately tactical.

## Where to use Cialdini specifically

- Auditing any sales asset against the 7 principles.
- Crafting opt-in language (Commitment).
- Designing testimonial systems (Social Proof).
- Building authority/credentialing language.
- Pre-Suasion language at the top of email/ads/landing pages.
- Ethics audits — making sure you're not crossing a line.

## Where NOT to use Cialdini alone

- Offer architecture → Hormozi.
- Funnel + sales structure → Brunson, Suby.
- Headline mechanics → Schwartz.
- Identity / story → Brunson, Robbins.
