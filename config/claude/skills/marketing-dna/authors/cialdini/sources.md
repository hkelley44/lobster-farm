## Academic papers worth searching (free on Google Scholar)

Cialdini has published widely. Key papers:
- "Harnessing the Science of Persuasion" (Harvard Business Review, 2001) — accessible summary.
- "A Focus Theory of Normative Conduct" (1990) — foundational social proof work.
- Various co-authored papers on commitment, scarcity, authority — search "Cialdini" on Google Scholar for current free access.

## Caveat

Pre-Suasion is the framework least well-covered by public summaries. The principle is clear (attention as leverage) but the book has dozens of specific tactical applications that don't surface in summaries. See `GAPS.md` for the recommendation.

## Still-unverified

- The behavioral-commitment 8-month duration in the McCain flag study is widely cited but I didn't independently verify against the original academic paper (it's from Carter, Ferguson, & Hassin's 2011 study, "A Single Exposure to the American Flag Shifts Support Toward Republicanism Up to 8 Months Later"). Cite as "study reported in Pre-Suasion" not as primary research.
