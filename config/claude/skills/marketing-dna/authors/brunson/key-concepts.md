# Russell Brunson — Key Concepts + Vocabulary

## Vocabulary

- **Funnel** — a specific sequence of pages designed to take a visitor through one specific outcome. Not a website.
- **Funnel Hacking** — going through competitors' funnels as a buyer and reverse-engineering.
- **Hook-Story-Offer (HSO)** — universal copy formula.
- **Value Ladder** — the staircase of offers.
- **Funnel Stacking** — multiple funnels chained.
- **Dream 100** — 100 people who already have your dream customers' attention.
- **Attractive Character** — the personality at the front of the brand.
- **Epiphany Bridge** — story structure that creates belief.
- **Big Domino** — the one belief that, if won, wins the sale.
- **False Beliefs (Vehicle/Internal/External)** — three categories of objection.
- **Perfect Webinar** — 90-min one-to-many sales framework.
- **The Stack** — the on-screen visualization of bundled value, item by item, on a webinar slide or sales page.
- **The Soap Opera Sequence** — email sequence based on cliffhangers + open loops, popularized by Brunson (originally from Andre Chaperon).
- **Seinfeld Emails** — daily emails about "nothing" (your day, opinion, anecdote) that always tie to a CTA. Builds connection.
- **New Opportunity** — Brunson's term for what you sell. NOT a better version of what they've tried — a NEW vehicle. This is Schwartz Sophistication Stage 1/2 territory.
- **Improvement vs New Opportunity** — Brunson says NEVER sell "improvement" (10% better diet), always sell "new opportunity" (this completely different way of eating). Improvement-positioned offers convert poorly.

## Thinking patterns

- **Identity > information.** People buy who they want to BECOME. The product is a vehicle.
- **Story-first.** Stories build belief. Belief moves people. Facts don't.
- **Build the movement.** Brand is a tribe. ClickFunnels Funnel Hackers are a tribe. Apple users are a tribe. You want a tribe.
- **Sequence the asks.** Never ask for the big yes on cold traffic. Climb the ladder.
- **One funnel = one product = one outcome.** Don't try to do everything on one page.
- **Polarity is profitable.** Have an enemy. Take positions. Vanilla doesn't convert.

## Brunson's biases to be aware of

- Religious about funnels (sells funnel software — of course). Some products don't need a 7-step funnel. SaaS in particular often does fine with a single landing → signup → product.
- Info-product / coaching world is his native medium. Brilliant there. Less native in B2B enterprise, in commodity DTC, in pure analytics SaaS.
- Heavy "movement" energy — works for some brands, comes across cult-y for others. CombatCall is closer to "tool" than "movement," so use Brunson sparingly here vs Hormozi.

## Where to use Brunson specifically

- Funnel architecture decisions (especially info-product / coaching offers).
- Story-heavy sales pages and emails.
- Building the founder's personal brand into the marketing.
- Audience-borrowing strategy (Dream 100).
- Naming + framing a "new opportunity" vs an improvement.

## Where NOT to use Brunson alone

- Pure-data tool offerings → too much story dilutes the math signal. Hormozi + Schwartz are better.
- Influence science → Cialdini.
- Awareness/sophistication analysis → Schwartz.
- Operator math → Hormozi.
