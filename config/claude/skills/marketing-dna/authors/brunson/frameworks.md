# Russell Brunson — Frameworks

Russell Brunson is co-founder of ClickFunnels and author of the "Secrets Trilogy" — *DotCom Secrets* (funnels), *Expert Secrets* (movements/identity), *Traffic Secrets* (audience). His superpower: the integration of *story* and *identity* into the funnel — turning customers into followers and followers into raving fans.

## 1. Hook, Story, Offer

**What it is.** Brunson's universal copywriting formula. Works for ads, emails, sales pages, videos, webinars. He says: "If something's not working in your funnel, it's always your hook, your story, or your offer. Every time."

- **Hook** — grab attention. Headline + first frame. The bait on the fishing line.
- **Story** — connect emotionally. Show how this changed your life or someone else's. Brunson's argument: facts tell, stories sell.
- **Offer** — present the deal. Price/value ratio so good it's hard to refuse.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Hook: *"I bought picks from supposed experts. They were guessing. So I built my own UFC model. My bankroll's been up since — and I'm giving you the first three picks of every card free."*
- Story: Cole's actual story — bought picks from tipsters, watched them fail (gut feeling dressed up as analysis), started building his own model 3–4 months ago, now runs his own bets off it, sharing first three free.
- Offer: "Three free fights every card. $25/mo or $120/yr for the full thing. Cancel anytime."
- *Story discipline:* no dollar specifics. Cole hasn't tracked exact P&L. "Data over feels" is the spine, not a recovery number.

**Source.** *DotCom Secrets*, *Expert Secrets*, *Traffic Secrets* — Brunson refers to HSO in all three. ClickFunnels blog: https://www.clickfunnels.com/blog/hook-story-offer/

## 2. The Value Ladder

**What it is.** A staircase of offers, lowest-cost at the bottom, highest-cost at the top. Each rung delivers value AND warms the customer for the next rung. You don't try to sell the top-shelf product to a stranger. You bring them in cheap, deliver, then ascend.

**Typical stages.**
1. **Bait / Lead Magnet** ($0 — free download, free quiz, free chapter)
2. **Front End** ($1-$47 — tripwire, low-friction first purchase)
3. **Middle** ($47-$497 — core product)
4. **Back End** ($497-$5000+ — premium / coaching / community)
5. **The Peak** ($5K+ — done-for-you / mastermind)

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Bait: free 3 fights/event (existing).
- Front End: $25/mo monthly sub — the friction-light entry.
- Middle: $120/yr annual sub — the commitment tier.
- Back End (FUTURE): a $300-500 "Bankroll Mastery" course + Discord community. Teaches bankroll math, +EV theory, fight-by-fight breakdowns. Coaching-adjacent. High margin.
- Peak (FUTURE): a $2-5K private syndicate / "tip line" / VIP fight-week Zoom calls. Restricted seats. Only for verified bettors of size.

**Source.** *DotCom Secrets*, Section 1. https://www.clickfunnels.com/blog/value-ladder/

## 4. The Dream 100

**What it is.** Identify the 100 people / brands / podcasts / publications / influencers who ALREADY have your dream customer's attention. Don't try to build an audience from scratch. Borrow theirs.

**Two strategies to enter the Dream 100:**
- **Work your way in (organic):** comment, engage, add value over months. Build a real relationship.
- **Buy your way in (paid):** sponsor their podcast, run ads to their audience, pay for a feature.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- MMA Dream 100 examples: Luke Thomas, Chael Sonnen, MMA Hour with Ariel Helwani, Bloody Elbow, MMA Fighting, r/MMA, r/sportsbook, r/MMAbetting, Spinnin Backfist, Heavy Hands podcast, MMA Twitter accounts w/ 50K+ that publicly track bets (Cole Shelton, etc.).
- Organic moves: comment intelligently on every fight-week thread, build a name on r/MMAbetting by posting verified pick records, write quality threads on X.
- Paid moves: sponsor an MMA podcast episode ($500-$2K range probably). Run X ads to Luke Thomas's followers via topic targeting.

**Source.** *Traffic Secrets*, Section 1. https://marketingsecrets.com/blog/the-dream-100-finally-explained-and-the-manana-principle

## 5. The Attractive Character

**What it is.** People follow PEOPLE, not brands. The "attractive character" is the personality at the front of your business. Doesn't have to be the founder — but it has to be a real, opinionated, flawed, identifiable human.

**Four components (per Brunson):**
1. **Backstory** — how you got here. Vulnerability matters.
2. **Parables / stories** — recurring metaphors and anecdotes that teach.
3. **Character flaws** — admit a weakness. Builds trust.
4. **Polarity** — take positions. Have enemies. Don't be vanilla.

**Plot lines** — Brunson teaches 4 recurring story arcs the AC uses: loss/redemption, us vs them, before/after, discovery.

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Cole IS the Attractive Character. He's the bettor who got tired of losing and built the tool. That's the story.
- Backstory: the specific bet that broke him.
- Parable: the time the model said one thing and his gut said another, and the model was right.
- Flaw: he's lost money too. He's not a Vegas wiseguy. He's a bettor with a better tool.
- Polarity: he has enemies — pick-selling Discord scammers, "lock of the day" tweets with no track record, sportsbooks that minimize action.

**Source.** *DotCom Secrets*, Section 2 + *Expert Secrets*, Section 1.

## 6. The Epiphany Bridge

**What it is.** A story structure for getting prospects to BELIEVE in your new opportunity. Walks them through the emotional experience that got YOU excited about the thing — so they get excited too.

**Structure.**
1. Backstory — where you were before.
2. Desire — what you wanted.
3. The wall — what blocked you (old vehicle failed).
4. The epiphany — the moment you discovered the new opportunity.
5. The plan — what you did with it.
6. The conflict — what almost stopped you.
7. The achievement — the outcome.
8. The transformation — who you became.
9. The mission — why you're sharing this.

**Applied — CombatCall.** Cole's origin story IS the Epiphany Bridge. Used at the top of the long-form sales page and in the welcome email.

**Source.** *Expert Secrets*, Section 1, "Secret #6: The Epiphany Bridge."

## 7. The Big Domino

**What it is.** Every sales presentation, sales page, webinar should be designed to knock down ONE belief — the Big Domino. The single thing that, if the prospect believes it, all other objections fall away.

For Brunson's own business it's: "Funnels are the future of online marketing, and ClickFunnels is the easiest way to build them."

**Applied — CombatCall.** Big Domino candidates:
- "UFC betting can be profitable with the right data and discipline."
- "Most people lose at UFC betting because they're betting on hype, not numbers."
- "If you had access to verified picks backed by data, your bankroll trajectory changes."

Test which one converts. Probably "betting on hype, not numbers" — it indicts what they currently do, then offers the alternative.

**Source.** *Expert Secrets*, Section 2.

## 8. False Beliefs (Vehicle / Internal / External)

**What it is.** Three categories of objections the prospect carries.

- **Vehicle false beliefs:** "This kind of product doesn't work." (CombatCall: "Pick services are all scams.")
- **Internal false beliefs:** "I can't do this." (CombatCall: "I'm not disciplined enough to bet long-term.")
- **External false beliefs:** "The world won't let me." (CombatCall: "Sportsbooks will limit my action.")

Your funnel must hit all three. Sales page sections, email sequence themes, ad creative variations should each take one belief and reverse it.

**Source.** *Expert Secrets*, Section 2.

## 9. The Perfect Webinar (One-to-Many Selling)

**What it is.** Brunson's 90-minute webinar framework. Even if you'll never run a webinar, the structure is the gold standard for any long-form sales asset (long video ad, long sales page, sales call):

1. **Introduction + big promise** (5 min)
2. **Origin story / Epiphany Bridge** (15 min)
3. **Teach the ONE thing** — the mechanism (45 min). Reverse the 3 false beliefs in sequence.
4. **The stack** — the offer reveal (15 min). Bundle, name, price, value-stack.
5. **Close + Q&A** (10 min). Restate. Risk reversal. Scarcity.

**Applied — CombatCall.** Don't need a webinar yet. But the structure maps onto the long-form sales page perfectly. Used in `templates/landing-pages/sales-page.md`.

**Source.** *Expert Secrets*, Section 3.

## 11. The Voice Evolution — Five Phases of Becoming an Expert

From Brunson's 2024-2025 thinking on how experts and entrepreneurs FIND their voice and then SELL their frameworks. This is the meta-framework that runs above DotCom/Expert/Traffic Secrets — it's about Tristan's-stage-of-career, not about the funnel.

1. **Dreamer** — initial spark. You're interested. You haven't done anything yet. You consume voraciously. Most people stay here forever.
2. **Reporter** — you learn from MULTIPLE experts. You synthesize. Avoid single-source bias. You're building inventory.
3. **Framework Creator** — you BUILD your own proprietary frameworks. You name them. You give them visual identity. This is the inflection point.
4. **Servant** — you test your frameworks on REAL CLIENTS, FOR FREE. You're not yet selling — you're stress-testing across industries and contexts.
5. **Expert / Guide** — you now sell the validated framework. The free work in Phase 4 generated proof, testimonials, and case studies. Now you charge.

**The rule.** "Just because you have a framework does not mean you should go start selling it." Validate first.

**Applied — Tristan.** Tristan as an archetype is post-Phase-3 — frameworks exist (the synthesis of six lineages). The question is whether each new framework Tristan builds for a specific CombatCall problem gets validated (Phase 4) before being shipped as a polished asset (Phase 5).

## 12. Teaching vs Selling a Framework — Different Recipes

Brunson's distinction between teaching and selling the SAME framework.

**When TEACHING (free content, top of funnel):**
1. Story (how YOU earned the framework — the lived experience that produced it)
2. Strategy (overview of the framework)
3. Tactics (step-by-step how)
4. Social proof (case studies)

**When SELLING (paid asset, sales page, webinar pitch):**
1. Story
2. Strategy
3. Social proof
4. (SKIP the tactics)

**Why skip tactics in sales mode.** Buyers are purchasing the FRAMEWORK NAME and the IMPLEMENTATION SHORTCUT. If you teach all the tactics for free, there's nothing left to buy. If you teach NONE of the tactics, the prospect doesn't trust the framework works. The "skip tactics, keep social proof" recipe creates the gap between proof-of-concept (trust) and proof-of-execution (the paid bridge).

**Applied — CombatCall.**
- Free X content / Reddit posts / YouTube: teach the FULL methodology of +EV identification. Give away the philosophy.
- Sales page / paywall copy: lead with the philosophy AND the social proof. Don't reveal the model weights, feature engineering, or specific code paths. Those are the "tactics" being sold.

**Source.** marketingsecrets.com.

## 16. The Framework-Naming Heuristic (Brunson's craft)

Across his recent talks, Brunson is consistent: frameworks WITH NAMES sell. Frameworks WITHOUT names get reinvented as common knowledge.

**The naming heuristic.**
- 3-5 syllables (memorable)
- Visual / metaphor-friendly ("Value Ladder," "Big Domino," "Dream 100")
- Owned (you defined it, it's yours)
- Numbered if applicable ("the 4-step X" — number creates feel of system)

**Applied — CombatCall.** When Tristan synthesizes the in-house betting model into a sellable concept, NAME IT. Candidates:
- "The Edge Score" — short, specific, memorable.
- "The Power-Accuracy Index" — descriptive, mechanism-revealing.
- "The +EV Algorithm" — owned terminology that becomes the brand vocabulary.

Use ONE name everywhere. Don't dilute it with synonyms. The name IS the moat (Schwartz "Mechanization," Brunson "Naming," Cialdini "Pre-suasive priming" — all pointing at the same lever).

**Source.** Brunson's recurring teaching across The Russell Brunson Show and Funnel Hacking Live keynotes.
