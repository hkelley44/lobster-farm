# Eugene Schwartz — Sources

## The Book (paid, hard to access — NOT pirated)

- **Breakthrough Advertising** (1966) by Eugene M. Schwartz. Out of print until Brian Kurtz re-published it. Current availability:
  - https://breakthroughadvertisingbook.com (Brian Kurtz's official republish; ~$125+)
  - https://www.amazon.com/Breakthrough-Advertising-Eugene-M-Schwartz/dp/0887232981 (used copies, $200-$500)

This book is the primary blind spot in our library. Public summaries cover the major frameworks (Awareness Stages, Sophistication, Mass Desire), but the 38 headline techniques and many subtler insights live only in the original. See `GAPS.md` for the recommendation re: purchase.

## Secondary sources / commentary worth knowing

- **Gary Halbert's letters** — Halbert wrote about Schwartz at length. Archives: https://www.thegaryhalbertletter.com (some letters reference Schwartz directly).
- **Brian Kurtz** — re-published Breakthrough Advertising. His own writing on Schwartz is the most authoritative secondary source. https://briankurtz.net
- **John Carlton** — direct disciple of the Schwartz school. https://john-carlton.com (sporadic).

## Schwartz biography

Schwartz wrote for industries from health to publishing. His personal "work day" was famously a series of timed 33-minute writing sprints — useful pattern for any copywriter to model.

## Caveat

More than any other author here, Schwartz framework write-ups depend heavily on secondary sources because the primary source is hard to access. Quote sparingly and attribute carefully. Where summaries disagree, default to the more conservative reading.
